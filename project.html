Project Overview

This project examines how artificial intelligence is transforming the U.S. consumer lending industry, a large and mature sector that still experiences rapid technological change. Using data and reports from the Federal Reserve, McKinsey, TransUnion, and other industry sources, the project tracks how AI-driven underwriting, alternative data, and automation are reshaping credit access, risk management, and firm strategy.

Rather than treating consumer lending as an ‚Äúemerging‚Äù industry, the project argues that AI is layered onto a highly regulated, structurally mature market. AI tools create targeted efficiency gains, new products such as BNPL, and shifting competitive dynamics between incumbent banks and fintech lenders. At the same time, they raise concerns about bias, workforce change, and systemic risk.
